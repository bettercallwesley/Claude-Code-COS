## 4. EATON'S ASBESTOS LITIGATION LANDSCAPE

### Portfolio Characteristics
- **Long-tail exposure:** New filings still occur due to 20-50 year latency periods for mesothelioma and asbestosis.
- **Multi-product exposure:** Brake systems, electrical equipment, and industrial compounds create multiple claim vectors.
- **Multi-jurisdiction:** Cases filed in CA, PA, IL, TX, and other states. Madison/St. Clair Counties (IL) and Cook County (IL) are known Judicial Hellholes for asbestos defendants.
- **High volume, pattern-based:** Hundreds of similar claims across jurisdictions -- ideal for portfolio intelligence.
- **Defense cost pressure:** Estimated $50K-$200K+ per case in defense costs. At scale, even marginal efficiency gains are worth millions.
- **Multi-firm management challenge:** If Eaton uses firms beyond D&E, data is fragmented across defense firms by jurisdiction.

### Notable Case History
1. **Stillman v. Eaton (2011):** Mechanic diagnosed with mesothelioma from asbestos brake exposure. Eaton was last defendant standing at trial. Settled during jury deliberations. Shows the real trial risk.
2. **Gorton v. Eaton (2017-2022):** Estate claim from Cutler-Hammer motor controller exposure (1959-1961). Summary judgment denied. Claims included fraudulent concealment -- meaning plaintiff alleged Eaton knew and hid asbestos risks.
3. **Estes v. Eaton (2020):** Navy electrician claimed Cutler-Hammer shipyard products caused mesothelioma. Eaton won this one. Shows they do win cases but the defense cost is still significant.
4. **$293M Arbitration (2018):** Eaton subsidiaries ordered to pay $293M related to a PepsiCo unit's asbestos settlement dispute. Signals the financial magnitude of legacy exposure.
