Most litigation departments have dashboards.

Very few have control.

There is a difference between knowing you have 4,200 open matters and knowing which 30 of them are going to blow up your Q3 results.

Visibility tells you what happened. Intelligence tells you what to do about it.

I talk to General Counsel and VP Claims leaders every week who can pull up beautiful reports. Color-coded. Filterable. Exportable.

And none of it changes a single outcome.

The insurance litigation market does not have a data problem. It has a decision-making problem.

The carriers winning right now are not the ones with the best dashboards. They are the ones who turned litigation data into operational leverage.

That is the difference between litigation management and litigation intelligence.
